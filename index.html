Bitmaker

Languages used : HTML, JavaScript, CSS

The first JavaScript project that truly challenged me. The user uses their mouseclicks to draw an image on the canvas. They are able to control what color they use, whether the gridlines are on, and select fun options like "gamer mode" (the colors become randomized) and a "gradient mode" that adds 0.1 opacity per click. The user can also resize their canvas up to 100 squares. This is done by dynamically altering the divs within the canvas, and reattaching event listeners. I also made the fonts!

FAQ

Q: Why Web Development?

A: I've had the privilege of working many kinds of jobs, living in many different places, and living many different lifestyles. I have had a great deal of time to consider what I want out of a career, and I have found what I'm looking for in web development. My interest was sparked by two things: My discovery of my love of coding, and my realization that it is important for me to have something to show for time invested in a project. Web development hits both of those two points while also allowing me to be creative both in my approach to aesthetics and problem solving -- I only recently realized how deeply important the latter is to me.

Q: Why is this program a good fit for me?

A: After deciding on a web development career as my goal, I began looking for a program that would best help me accomplish that goal. The fast-track Software Engineering Technician program at Centennial seemed perfectly aligned with this. While I have done much on my own to put me closer to my professional goals, I'm confident the structure provided by Centennial would help push my knowledge to the next level and help prepare me for the workplace. The two-semester time period in tandem with the material covered gives me the assurance that this program is the best fit for me.

Q: Why do I believe I am a good fit for this program?

A: I have studied on my own for the past year. I reviewed what material is covered in the first semester of the program and made a list of topics I needed to know. I studied SQL, JavaScript (and some associated libraries), Java, HTML, CSS, and the SDLC to adequately prepare. In the past several months I have spent six to ten hours a day studying these topics to build the strongest case for my enrollment in the program. I found syllabi and textbooks for some of the prerequisite courses, and worked through them (as was the case with SQL), enrolled in courses through Centennial (as I did to learn Java). I enrolled in online courses where I could not find course information. I am confident that my knowledge and background is enough for me to excel at Centennial. I believe that after reviewing this website you will too.
